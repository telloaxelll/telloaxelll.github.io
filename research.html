Research

Pandey Lab | Analyzing Transformer Frameworks for ACC via Online Parameter Estimator Methods

Aug 2024 – Present   |   University of California, Merced

Investigating Transformer architectures, specifically the Meta Output Predictor (MOP) framework, to learn and approximate complex dynamical systems. These systems, often difficult to model due to high dimensionality and time-dependence, are evaluated for their scalability and real-world adaptability. Traditional state-space and differential equation methods are used as comparative baselines.

Skills: Python · High Performance Computing (HPC) · Machine Learning · PyTorch

May 2024 – Aug 2024   |   The National Institutes of Health

Contributed to disease spread modeling using a hybrid agent-based and kinetic Monte Carlo simulation of the SIR model. Investigated stochastic effects and parameter sensitivity in epidemic progression under different public health scenarios.



Projects

Applied Mathematics Challenge | Ecological Time-Series Modeling with ODEs

Mar 2025 – Present   |   University of California, Merced

Collaborated in a team to model and forecast bobcat and rabbit populations in Merced County using sparse observational data over a 90-day period. Applied systems of ordinary differential equations (predator-prey models) to simulate ecological dynamics and predict population trends. Interpolated missing time-series data using cubic spline and linear interpolation methods. Fit a predator-prey model using least squares to estimate model parameters from noisy observational data. Used finite difference methods and numerical solvers (solve_ivp) to simulate forward dynamics. Evaluated whether the bobcat population would exceed critical thresholds requiring intervention.

Skills: Pandas · NumPy · Python · Data Analysis · Matplotlib
